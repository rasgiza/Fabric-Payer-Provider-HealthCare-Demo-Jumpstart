## 1. Formulary Overview

The Lakeside Health System formulary is organized into **4 tiers** based on clinical evidence, cost-effectiveness, and payer contract alignment. Prescribers should select the lowest tier medication that is clinically appropriate.

### 1.1 Tier Structure

Tier structure follows **USP Medicare Model Guidelines** (United States Pharmacopeia) and **CMS Part D Formulary Requirements** (42 CFR 423.120). Drug reference data validated against the **FDA Orange Book** (Approved Drug Products with Therapeutic Equivalence Evaluations) and **RxNorm** (NLM/NIH).

| Tier | Description | Patient Copay (Typical) | Prior Auth |
|------|------------|------------------------|-----------|
| **Tier 1 — Preferred Generic** | First-line generic medications | $5-15 | No |
| **Tier 2 — Non-Preferred Generic** | Alternative generics, less evidence or higher cost | $15-30 | Rarely |
| **Tier 3 — Preferred Brand** | Brand medications when no generic available | $30-75 | Sometimes |
| **Tier 4 — Specialty** | High-cost, specialty, or injectable medications | $75-200+ | Always |

## 6. Medication Adherence Monitoring

### 6.1 Proportion of Days Covered (PDC) Targets

PDC targets per **NCQA HEDIS Medication Adherence Measures** (MY 2025). These three adherence measures are **triple-weighted in CMS Star Ratings** (per CMS Part C & D Star Ratings Technical Notes, October 2024).

| Drug Class | PDC Target | HEDIS/Stars Measure |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| ACE/ARB (Lisinopril, Losartan) | ≥ 80% | RAS Antagonist Adherence |
| Statins (Atorvastatin) | ≥ 80% | Statin Adherence |
| Diabetes (Metformin, Glipizide, Insulin) | ≥ 80% | Diabetes Medication Adherence |
| Antidepressants (Sertraline, Escitalopram) | ≥ 80% for acute phase (84 days) | Antidepressant Medication Management |

### 6.2 Non-Adherence Interventions
- **PDC < 60%:** Pharmacist outreach call within 7 days
- **PDC < 40%:** Prescriber notified, care coordinator engaged
- **PDC < 20%:** Flag for care management, assess barriers (cost, side effects, health literacy)

### 6.3 Clinical Consequences of Non-Adherence by Therapeutic Area

When evaluating non-adherent patients, clinicians should be aware of the specific clinical risks associated with missed doses by drug class. Per **AHA/ACC 2022** (Heidenreich et al., *Circulation* 2022;145:e895-e1032), **ADA Standards of Care 2024** (*Diabetes Care* Vol. 47, Suppl. 1), and **ATA Guidelines 2014** (Jonklaas et al., *Thyroid* 2014;24(12):1670-1751):

| Drug Class | Non-Adherence Clinical Consequence | Weight Impact | Specialty to Notify |
|---|---|---|---|
| Loop Diuretic (Furosemide) | Fluid retention, pulmonary congestion, decompensated CHF | **Rapid weight gain: 2-5 lbs in 24-72 hours** | Cardiology |
| ACE Inhibitor (Lisinopril) | Worsening cardiac remodeling, fluid overload | **Gradual weight gain: 1-3 lbs/week** from fluid retention | Cardiology |
| Beta Blocker (Metoprolol) | Rebound tachycardia, arrhythmia, HF decompensation | Weight fluctuation from hemodynamic instability | Cardiology |
| Biguanide (Metformin) | Uncontrolled hyperglycemia, insulin resistance | **Metabolic weight gain: 2-4 lbs/month** due to impaired glucose utilization | Endocrinology |
| Insulin (Glargine) | Severe hyperglycemia, DKA risk | Paradoxical weight loss from uncontrolled diabetes (catabolic state) | Endocrinology |
| Thyroid Hormone (Levothyroxine) | Hypothyroid state, metabolic slowdown | **Weight gain: 2-5 lbs/month** from decreased metabolic rate | Endocrinology |
| SSRI (Sertraline/Escitalopram) | Mood destabilization, depression relapse | **Emotional eating, weight gain** from untreated depression; non-compliance cascade affecting all medications | Psychiatry |
| Benzodiazepine (Lorazepam) | Withdrawal syndrome, rebound anxiety | Stress-related weight changes | Psychiatry |
| Statin (Atorvastatin) | Accelerated atherosclerosis (no acute symptoms) | No direct weight impact | Cardiology/Internal Medicine |
| Anticoagulant (Warfarin) | Thromboembolic events (stroke, PE, DVT) | No direct weight impact | Anticoagulation clinic |

**Key Insight for Care Managers:** When a patient is non-adherent to multiple drug classes, the weight gain effects COMPOUND. For example, a CHF patient non-adherent to both Furosemide AND Lisinopril may experience 5-8 lbs of weight gain within one week — a clinical emergency requiring immediate Cardiology escalation and home health visit within 24 hours per CMS TCM 99495/99496.
