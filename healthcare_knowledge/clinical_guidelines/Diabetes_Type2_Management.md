# Lakeside Health System — Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus Management Guidelines

**ICD-10:** E11.9 — Type 2 diabetes mellitus without complications  
**Related Codes:** E78.5 (Hyperlipidemia), I10 (Hypertension), N18.3 (CKD Stage 3)  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Approved By:** Chief Medical Officer, Lakeside Health System  
**Applicable Facilities:** All Lakeside facilities (FAC001-FAC008)

---

## 1. Scope

These guidelines apply to all adult patients (age ≥ 18) diagnosed with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus across all encounter types (inpatient, outpatient, emergency, observation, telehealth) at Lakeside Health System.

## 2. Screening and Diagnosis

### 2.1 Screening Criteria
Per USPSTF Grade B Recommendation (*JAMA* 2021;326(8):736-743):
- All patients aged 35-70 with BMI ≥ 25
- All patients with risk factors: family history, gestational diabetes, PCOS, hypertension (I10)
- Annual screening for patients on atypical antipsychotics

### 2.2 Diagnostic Criteria
| Test | Diabetes | Prediabetes |
|------|----------|-------------|
| HbA1c | ≥ 6.5% | 5.7-6.4% |
| Fasting glucose | ≥ 126 mg/dL | 100-125 mg/dL |
| 2-hour OGTT | ≥ 200 mg/dL | 140-199 mg/dL |

### 2.3 Required Workup at Diagnosis
- **Labs:** Comprehensive metabolic panel (CPT 80053), Complete blood count (CPT 85025), HbA1c, Lipid panel, Urine albumin/creatinine ratio
- **Review:** Cardiovascular risk assessment (concurrent I10, E78.5, I25.10)

## 3. Glycemic Targets

| Population | HbA1c Target | Fasting Glucose | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|-------|
| Most adults | < 7.0% | 80-130 mg/dL | Standard target |
| Young, healthy, newly diagnosed | < 6.5% | 80-110 mg/dL | If achievable without hypoglycemia |
| Elderly (>75), complex comorbidities | < 8.0% | 100-150 mg/dL | Avoid hypoglycemia |
| End-stage CKD (N18.3+) | < 8.0% | Individualize | Avoid hypoglycemia |

## 4. Pharmacotherapy — Stepwise Approach

### Step 1: Monotherapy
- **Metformin 500 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 860974) — FIRST-LINE for all patients
  - Starting dose: 500 mg once daily with evening meal
  - Titrate: 500 mg every 1-2 weeks to max 2000 mg/day (divided BID)
  - **Contraindications:** eGFR < 30, active liver disease, metabolic acidosis
  - Monitor: Renal function every 3-6 months, B12 levels annually
  - **Hold 48 hours before and after contrast imaging** (CT abdomen CPT 74177)

### Step 2: Dual Therapy (if HbA1c remains > 7% after 3 months)
Add one of the following based on clinical profile. Per ADA Standards of Care (ElSayed et al., *Diabetes Care* 2024;47(Suppl 1):S158-S178), SGLT2 inhibitors and GLP-1 receptor agonists are now **preferred Step 2 agents** for patients with HF, CKD, or ASCVD, regardless of HbA1c. This is also supported by the ADA-EASD Consensus Report (Davies et al., *Diabetes Care* 2022;45(11):2753-2786):

| Agent | Best For | Caution | ADA Priority |
|-------|----------|--------|-------------|
| **SGLT2 Inhibitor** | CHF (I50.9), CKD (N18.3), eGFR >= 20 | Genital infections, DKA risk | **Preferred** for HF/CKD |
| **GLP-1 Receptor Agonist** | Obesity (BMI >= 30), ASCVD (I25.10), weight reduction needed | GI side effects, pancreatitis | **Preferred** for ASCVD/Obesity |
| Glipizide 5 MG (RxNorm 311040) | Cost-sensitive patients without HF, CKD, or ASCVD | Hypoglycemia risk, weight gain | Alternative when SGLT2/GLP-1 not indicated or not tolerated |

> **Clinical Decision:** If patient has HF, CKD (eGFR < 60), or established ASCVD, start SGLT2 or GLP-1 as Step 2 — do NOT default to sulfonylurea. Sulfonylureas remain appropriate for cost-sensitive patients without these comorbidities.

- **Glipizide 5 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 311040) — Sulfonylurea option (use when SGLT2/GLP-1 not indicated)
  - Starting dose: 5 mg daily before breakfast
  - Max: 20 mg/day (divided)
  - **High hypoglycemia risk** — educate patient, especially elderly
  - Avoid in patients with irregular meal patterns

### Step 3: Triple Therapy or Insulin
- **Insulin Glargine 100 UNT/ML Injectable Solution** (RxNorm 1373463) — Basal insulin
  - Starting dose: 10 units at bedtime OR 0.1-0.2 units/kg/day
  - Titrate: Increase by 2-4 units every 3 days based on fasting glucose
  - Target fasting glucose: 80-130 mg/dL
  - **Requires prior authorization** with most payers if HbA1c < 8% without oral failure documentation
  - Patient education: injection technique, hypoglycemia management, sick day rules

## 5. Comorbidity-Specific Management

### 5.1 Hypertension (I10) — Present in ~70% of T2DM patients
- Target BP: < 130/80 mmHg
- **Lisinopril 10 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 314076) — Preferred first-line (renoprotective)
- **Losartan 50 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 310798) — If ACE intolerant
- **Amlodipine 5 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 197361) — Add-on if target not met

### 5.2 Hyperlipidemia (E78.5)
- **Atorvastatin 20 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 200031) — ALL diabetic patients aged 40-75
  - Moderate intensity for primary prevention
  - High intensity (40-80 mg) if ASCVD present (I25.10)
- Monitor LDL annually; target < 100 mg/dL (< 70 if ASCVD)

### 5.3 Chronic Kidney Disease (N18.3)
- Screen annually: eGFR + urine albumin/creatinine ratio
- **Reduce Metformin** if eGFR 30-45; **stop** if eGFR < 30
- SGLT2 inhibitor recommended if eGFR ≥ 20
- Nephrology referral if eGFR < 30 or rapidly declining

### 5.4 Depression (F32.9)
- Screen with PHQ-9 at every diabetes visit (depression 2-3x more common in diabetes)
- **Sertraline 50 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 312938) — Preferred, weight-neutral
- Diabetes distress is distinct from clinical depression — assess both

### 5.5 Gastroparesis (GI complications)
- **Omeprazole 20 MG Delayed Release Oral Capsule** (RxNorm 311700) — For concurrent GERD (K21.0)
- Consider prokinetic agents for gastroparesis

## 7. Encounter Protocols

### 7.1 Outpatient Visits (CPT 99213-99215)
- HbA1c at each visit if above target, otherwise every 6 months
- Medication reconciliation — document adherence barriers
- Foot exam with monofilament at every visit
- Referrals: Ophthalmology, Podiatry, Diabetes Education, Nutrition

### 7.2 Emergency Department (CPT 99283-99285)
- Blood glucose on arrival for all diabetic patients
- DKA protocol if glucose > 300 + acidosis
- Hypoglycemia protocol if glucose < 54

### 7.3 Inpatient (CPT 99221-99223)
- Basal-bolus insulin regimen (discontinue oral agents)
- Blood glucose monitoring QAC and QHS (4x daily)
- Target glucose 140-180 mg/dL (inpatient)
- Endocrinology consult for glucose > 300 or DKA
- Resume oral medications 24-48 hours before discharge if stable

## 8. Medication Adherence Strategies

Adherence management follows NCQA HEDIS Measurement Year 2025 standards and ADA Standards of Care (ADA, *Diabetes Care* 2024;47(Suppl 1):S1-S321).

### 8.1 Adherence Targets
- **Proportion of Days Covered (PDC) ≥ 80%** for Metformin, statins, ACE/ARB — this is the NCQA HEDIS standard threshold. Patients below PDC 0.80 are classified as non-adherent per HEDIS MY 2025 methodology.
- PDC 0.50-0.80 = Partial adherence — requires pharmacist intervention
- PDC < 0.50 = Non-adherent — requires clinical pharmacist review and barrier assessment per ADA Section 5 (Facilitating Positive Health Behaviors)
- Monitor via pharmacy claims data in semantic model (dim_medication + fact_prescription)

### 8.2 Common Barriers and Interventions
Per ADA Standards of Care Section 5 (ElSayed et al., *Diabetes Care* 2024;47(Suppl 1):S77-S110) and CMS Diabetes Self-Management Training (DSMT) guidelines:

| Barrier | Evidence-Based Intervention | Source |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Cost | Switch to generic, 90-day supply, manufacturer assistance programs | CMS DSMT, ADA Section 5 |
| Side effects (Metformin GI) | Extended-release formulation, take with food, slow titration | ADA-EASD Consensus (Davies et al., 2022) |
| Insulin fear | Diabetes educator consult, demonstration, address myths | ADA Section 5: Facilitating Positive Health Behaviors |
| Complexity | Simplify regimen, pill organizer, pharmacy sync | NCQA HEDIS Medication Adherence measures |
| Depression (F32.9) | Treat depression first — depression is 2-3x more common in diabetes (Anderson et al., *Diabetes Care* 2001;24:1069-1078) | ADA Section 5 |
| Health literacy | Pictorial medication guides, teach-back method, interpreter services | AHRQ Health Literacy Universal Precautions Toolkit |

## 10. Quality Metrics (HEDIS / CMS Stars)

These metrics are defined by NCQA HEDIS Measurement Year 2025 and directly impact CMS Star Ratings (CMS Medicare Part C & D Star Ratings Technical Notes, 2025). Poor performance on diabetes measures reduces Star Rating, affecting Medicare Advantage reimbursement by up to 5%.

| Measure | Description | Target | Source |
|---------|-------------|--------|--------|
| HbA1c Testing (CDC) | % of diabetics with annual HbA1c test | ≥ 90% | HEDIS CDC, NCQA MY 2025 |
| HbA1c Poor Control | % with HbA1c > 9% (LOWER is better) | < 15% | HEDIS CDC, NCQA MY 2025 |
| HbA1c Good Control | % with HbA1c < 8% | ≥ 65% | HEDIS CDC, NCQA MY 2025 |
| Eye Exam (CDC) | Annual retinal exam | ≥ 60% | HEDIS CDC, USPSTF |
| Nephropathy Screening | Annual urine albumin test | ≥ 85% | HEDIS CDC, KDIGO 2024 |
| BP Control < 140/90 | % at target | ≥ 70% | HEDIS CBP, ACC/AHA 2017 |
| Statin Therapy | % on statin if age 40-75 | ≥ 80% | HEDIS SPC, ACC/AHA 2018 |
| Medication Adherence (PDC) | % with PDC ≥ 0.80 for diabetes meds | ≥ 85% | HEDIS Part D, NCQA MY 2025 |
