# Lakeside Health System — Congestive Heart Failure (CHF) Management Guidelines

**ICD-10:** I50.9 — Heart failure, unspecified  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Review Cycle:** Annual  
**Approved By:** Chief Medical Officer, Lakeside Health System  
**Applicable Facilities:** FAC001 (Lakeside Main Hospital), FAC002 (Lakeside Heart Center), FAC005 (Lakeside Community Clinic)

---

## 1. Scope

These guidelines are based on the **2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Guideline for the Management of Heart Failure** (Heidenreich PA et al., *Circulation*. 2022;145(18):e895-e1032) and the **2017 ACC/AHA/HFSA Focused Update** (Yancy CW et al., *J Am Coll Cardiol*. 2017;70(6):776-803). They apply to all patients aged 18+ presenting with CHF (ICD-10: I50.9) across inpatient, outpatient, emergency, and telehealth encounters at Lakeside Health System facilities in Michigan and Ohio.

## 2. Diagnosis and Classification

### 2.1 Initial Workup
- **Required labs:** Comprehensive metabolic panel (CPT 80053), Complete blood count (CPT 85025), BNP/NT-proBNP
- **Required imaging:** Chest X-ray 2 views (CPT 71046), Echocardiography complete (CPT 93306)
- **Required cardiac:** Electrocardiogram complete (CPT 93000)

### 2.2 NYHA Classification
| Class | Description | Action Level |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| I | No limitation of physical activity | Outpatient management |
| II | Slight limitation; comfortable at rest | Outpatient with cardiology referral |
| III | Marked limitation; comfortable only at rest | Urgent cardiology consult, consider observation |
| IV | Unable to carry on physical activity without discomfort | Inpatient admission required |

### 2.3 Ejection Fraction Categories
- **HFrEF** (EF ≤ 40%): Reduced ejection fraction — requires full GDMT
- **HFmrEF** (EF 41-49%): Mildly reduced — individualized therapy
- **HFpEF** (EF ≥ 50%): Preserved — focus on comorbidities and diuresis

## 3. First-Line Pharmacotherapy (Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy)

GDMT recommendations follow the **2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Guideline** (Heidenreich et al., *Circulation* 2022;145:e895-e1032). For treatment sequencing, see McMurray & Packer (*Circulation* 2021;143:875-877).

### 3.1 ACE Inhibitors / ARBs
- **Lisinopril 10 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 314076) — First-line for HFrEF
  - Starting dose: 5 mg daily, titrate to 20-40 mg daily
  - Monitor: Renal function, potassium at 1-2 weeks
- **Losartan 50 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 310798) — Alternative if ACE intolerant
  - Starting dose: 25 mg daily, titrate to 100 mg daily
  - Use when: ACE inhibitor cough, angioedema history

### 3.2 Beta Blockers
- **Metoprolol Succinate 50 MG Extended Release** (RxNorm 866924) — Evidence-based beta blocker for HF
  - Starting dose: 12.5-25 mg daily, titrate to 200 mg daily
  - **Do not initiate during acute decompensation**
  - Titrate every 2 weeks if tolerated

### 3.3 Diuretics
- **Furosemide 40 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 310429) — Loop diuretic for volume management
  - Adjust based on daily weights, symptoms, renal function
  - Target: Net negative 1-2L/day in acute decompensation
  - Transition to lowest effective maintenance dose at discharge

### 3.4 Anticoagulation (when indicated)
- **Warfarin Sodium 5 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 836585) — For concurrent atrial fibrillation
  - Target INR 2.0-3.0
  - Monitor weekly until stable, then monthly

### 3.5 Statin Therapy (concurrent atherosclerosis)
- **Atorvastatin 20 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 200031) — When concurrent I25.10 (atherosclerotic heart disease)
  - Consider in all CHF patients with coronary artery disease
  - Monitor LFTs at baseline and as clinically indicated

## 4. Comorbidity Management

### 4.1 Hypertension (I10)
- Target BP < 130/80 in CHF patients
- Lisinopril and Metoprolol are preferred dual agents (treats both HF and HTN)
- **Amlodipine 5 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 197361) — Safe add-on in HFrEF if additional BP control needed

### 4.2 Type 2 Diabetes (E11.9)
- **Metformin 500 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 860974) — Safe in stable CHF (avoid if eGFR < 30)
- SGLT2 inhibitors strongly recommended (reduces HF hospitalizations)
- Monitor renal function closely

### 4.3 Chronic Kidney Disease (N18.3)
- Adjust all renally-cleared medications
- Hold ACE/ARB if creatinine rises > 30% from baseline
- Coordinate with nephrology for stage 3+

### 4.4 Depression (F32.9)
- Screen all CHF patients with PHQ-9
- **Sertraline 50 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 312938) — Preferred SSRI in cardiac patients
- Avoid TCAs (cardiac risk)

## 5. Encounter-Based Protocols

### 5.1 Emergency Department (CPT 99283-99285)
- BNP, CMP, CBC, ECG, Chest X-ray within 60 minutes
- IV Furosemide if acute decompensation
- Echocardiogram if no prior or change in status
- Disposition: Admit if NYHA III-IV, O2 requirement, hemodynamic instability

### 5.2 Inpatient (CPT 99221-99223)
- Daily weights, strict I&O
- Telemetry monitoring
- Pharmacy-led medication reconciliation
- Cardiology consult within 24 hours for new diagnosis
- Transition to oral diuretics 24-48 hours before planned discharge
- Schedule follow-up within **7 days** of discharge (30-day readmission prevention)

### 5.3 Outpatient Follow-up (CPT 99213-99215)
- Post-discharge visit within 7 days
- Medication titration visit every 2-4 weeks until optimized
- Labs (CMP, BNP) at each titration visit
- Echocardiogram annually or with clinical change
- Remote patient monitoring/telehealth for stable NYHA I-II patients

## 6. Surgical Considerations

### 6.1 Coronary Revascularization
- **Coronary artery bypass, single graft** (CPT 33533) — For ischemic cardiomyopathy with viable myocardium
- Pre-op echo and cardiac catheterization required
- Multi-disciplinary heart team review mandatory

## 7. Readmission Prevention Bundle

| Intervention | Timing | Responsible |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| Medication reconciliation | Before discharge | Pharmacy |
| Patient/family education | Before discharge | Nursing + Heart Failure Educator |
| Follow-up appointment scheduled | Before discharge | Care Coordinator |
| Telehealth check-in | 48 hours post-discharge | RN Care Manager |
| Outpatient visit | Within 7 days | Cardiology/Primary Care |
| Home health referral | Day of discharge (if applicable) | Social Work |

## 8. Quality Metrics

Per CMS HRRP (42 CFR 412.150-154; ACA Section 3025) and NCQA HEDIS MY 2025:
- **30-day readmission rate target:** < 15% (CMS national average for HF: 21.9% per CMS Hospital Compare). Hospitals exceeding this face up to **3% Medicare payment reduction**.
- **Medication adherence (PDC):** ≥ 80% for ACE/ARB, Beta Blocker — per NCQA HEDIS Medication Adherence measures (triple-weighted in CMS Star Ratings)
- **7-day follow-up rate:** ≥ 85% — per CMS Transitional Care Management (TCM) codes 99495/99496
- **Average length of stay:** ≤ 5 days
- **Mortality rate:** Track and benchmark against national averages per CMS Hospital Compare

## 10. Clinical Consequences of Medication Non-Adherence in CHF Patients

Per the **2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Guideline** (Heidenreich et al., *Circulation* 2022;145:e895-e1032) and **CMS HRRP** (42 CFR 412.150-154), medication non-adherence in CHF patients leads to specific, measurable clinical deterioration. Care managers and providers MUST monitor for these consequences and escalate per the readmission prevention protocol.

### 10.1 Non-Adherence Consequence Map by Drug Class

| Drug Class | Medication | Non-Adherence Consequence | Clinical Sign | Timeframe | Escalation Required |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Loop Diuretic** | Furosemide 40 MG | **Fluid retention and rapid weight gain** (2-5 lbs in 24-72 hours) | Peripheral edema, dyspnea, elevated JVP, pulmonary congestion | 24-72 hours | **Cardiology notification within 24 hours**; home health visit per CMS TCM 99495/99496 |
| **ACE Inhibitor** | Lisinopril 10 MG | Worsening cardiac remodeling, increased afterload, fluid retention, **gradual weight gain** (1-3 lbs/week) | Rising blood pressure, increased dyspnea on exertion | 1-2 weeks | Cardiology follow-up within 7 days |
| **Beta Blocker** | Metoprolol Succinate 50 MG | Increased heart rate, arrhythmia risk, **rebound tachycardia and weight fluctuation** | Palpitations, exercise intolerance, anxiety | 48-72 hours (rebound) | **Do NOT abruptly discontinue**; cardiology consult for tapering |
| **ARB** | Losartan 50 MG | Same as ACE Inhibitor — worsening cardiac output, fluid retention, **weight gain** | Edema, elevated BNP | 1-2 weeks | Primary care or cardiology within 7 days |
| **Statin** | Atorvastatin 20 MG | Accelerated atherosclerosis, increased cardiovascular events | No immediate symptoms; long-term LDL elevation | Weeks to months | Review at next visit; reinforce education |
| **Anticoagulant** | Warfarin 5 MG | Thromboembolic events (stroke, DVT, PE) in patients with A-fib | INR subtherapeutic (<2.0) | 3-5 days | **Immediate** anticoagulation clinic notification |

### 10.2 Weight Gain as a Clinical Red Flag in CHF

Per the **2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Guideline** (Heidenreich et al., *Circulation* 2022;145:e895-e1032, Section 7.3.2 — Self-Care):

- **Daily weight monitoring is MANDATORY** for all CHF patients
- **Weight gain > 2 lbs in 24 hours** or **> 5 lbs in 1 week** = contact care team immediately
- The primary cause of rapid weight gain in CHF is **missed diuretic doses** (Furosemide non-adherence)
- Secondary contributors: high sodium intake, ACE/ARB non-adherence leading to fluid retention
- A CHF patient with PDC < 0.60 for Loop Diuretics is at **HIGH RISK for acute decompensation** requiring ED visit or readmission

**Clinical Action Protocol for Weight Gain in CHF Patients:**
1. **Identify non-adherent medications** — Check PDC scores for Loop Diuretic, ACE/ARB, Beta Blocker
2. **Notify Cardiology provider** — The patient's cardiologist (not ER, not primary care) should adjust diuretic dosing
3. **Schedule home health visit within 24 hours** — Per CMS Transitional Care Management (CPT 99495/99496)
4. **Medication therapy management (MTM)** — Pharmacist-led review per NCQA HEDIS MY 2025 to identify adherence barriers
5. **Assess social determinants** — Transportation, cost, health literacy may drive non-adherence (per ACA Section 3025, CMS Health Equity requirements)

### 10.3 Provider Routing for Non-Adherence Escalation

| Non-Adherent Drug Class | Responsible Specialty | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Loop Diuretic (Furosemide) | **Cardiology** | Adjust diuretic dose, assess volume status, schedule within 24h |
| ACE Inhibitor / ARB | **Cardiology** or **Internal Medicine** | Reassess dosing, check renal function, schedule within 7 days |
| Beta Blocker (Metoprolol) | **Cardiology** | NEVER abruptly stop — taper plan required, consult within 48h |
| SSRI (Sertraline) | **Psychiatry** | Mood destabilization, non-compliance cascade — schedule within 7 days |
| Benzodiazepine (Lorazepam) | **Psychiatry** | Withdrawal risk — taper plan required, consult within 48h |
| Insulin / Biguanide | **Endocrinology** or **Internal Medicine** | HbA1c deterioration, metabolic weight gain — schedule within 14 days |
| Thyroid Hormone (Levothyroxine) | **Endocrinology** | Hypothyroid weight gain (2-5 lbs/month), fatigue, cold intolerance — schedule within 14 days |

## 11. Documentation Requirements

- NYHA class documented at every encounter
- Ejection fraction documented on problem list
- Weight recorded at every visit
- Volume status assessment documented
- Medication reconciliation with reason for any GDMT not prescribed
- Patient education documented (diet, medication compliance, daily weights, when to call)
