# Lakeside Health System — COPD Management Guidelines

**ICD-10:** J44.9 — Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, unspecified  
**Exacerbation Code:** J44.1 — COPD with acute exacerbation  
**Related Codes:** J45.909 (Asthma), J06.9 (Upper respiratory infection), J18.9 (Pneumonia)  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Approved By:** Chief Medical Officer, Pulmonology Division Chair  
**Applicable Facilities:** All Lakeside facilities (FAC001-FAC008)

---

## 1. Scope

These guidelines cover diagnosis, stable management, and acute exacerbation treatment for adult patients with COPD across all Lakeside Health System encounter types.

## 2. Diagnosis and Classification

### 2.1 Spirometry Criteria
- COPD confirmed: Post-bronchodilator **FEV1/FVC < 0.70**
- Spirometry required for diagnosis — clinical suspicion alone is insufficient

### 2.2 GOLD Severity Classification
| Stage | FEV1 (% predicted) | Clinical |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| GOLD 1 — Mild | ≥ 80% | May be asymptomatic |
| GOLD 2 — Moderate | 50-79% | Dyspnea on exertion |
| GOLD 3 — Severe | 30-49% | Increased dyspnea, frequent exacerbations |
| GOLD 4 — Very Severe | < 30% | Severe dyspnea at rest, quality of life impaired |

### 2.3 ABCD Assessment (Symptom + Exacerbation Risk)
| Group | Symptoms | Exacerbation History | Initial Therapy |
|-------|----------|---------------------|-----------------|
| A | Low symptoms (mMRC 0-1, CAT < 10) | 0-1 per year, no hospitalization | Rescue bronchodilator |
| B | High symptoms (mMRC ≥ 2, CAT ≥ 10) | 0-1 per year, no hospitalization | LABA or LAMA |
| C | Low symptoms | ≥ 2 per year or ≥ 1 with hospitalization | LAMA |
| D | High symptoms | ≥ 2 per year or ≥ 1 with hospitalization | LAMA + LABA ± ICS |

### 2.4 Required Initial Workup
- **Labs:** Complete blood count (CPT 85025), Comprehensive metabolic panel (CPT 80053), Alpha-1 antitrypsin level
- **Imaging:** Chest X-ray 2 views (CPT 71046)
- **Advanced imaging:** CT abdomen and pelvis with contrast (CPT 74177) — if concern for malignancy or alternative diagnosis
- **Cardiac:** ECG (CPT 93000) — to evaluate for concurrent cardiac disease (I50.9, I25.10)

## 3. Stable COPD Pharmacotherapy

### 3.1 Rescue/Reliever Therapy
- **Albuterol 0.09 MG/ACTUAT Metered Dose Inhaler** (RxNorm 895994)
  - **ALL COPD patients** should have rescue albuterol
  - Use: 1-2 puffs every 4-6 hours PRN for shortness of breath
  - **Inhaler technique re-education at every visit**
  - If using > 2x per week (excluding exercise pre-treatment) → escalate therapy

### 3.2 Maintenance Controller Therapy
- **Fluticasone Propionate 110 MCG/ACTUAT Inhaler** (RxNorm 896188) — Inhaled corticosteroid
  - Indicated: Group D patients, eosinophilic phenotype (blood eos ≥ 300), frequent exacerbations
  - Dose: 1-2 puffs BID
  - **Rinse mouth after use** to prevent oral candidiasis
  - Monitor: Bone density if high-dose long-term use
  - **Not recommended as monotherapy** — always combine with LABA

### 3.3 Combination Therapy Recommendations
| Patient Profile | Recommended | Rationale |
|----------------|------------|-----------|
| Group B — symptomatic | LABA or LAMA monotherapy | Symptom control |
| Group D — persistent symptoms | LABA + LAMA | Dual bronchodilation |
| Group D — frequent exacerbations + eosinophilia | LABA + LAMA + ICS (Fluticasone 896188) | Triple therapy |
| Frequent exacerbations despite triple therapy | Add roflumilast or azithromycin prophylaxis | Specialist consultation |

### 3.4 Concurrent Asthma (Asthma-COPD Overlap — J45.909)
- Some patients carry both J44.9 and J45.909
- **ICS-containing regimen is mandatory** in overlap patients
- Albuterol (895994) for rescue + Fluticasone (896188) for maintenance as minimum

## 4. Comorbidity Management

### 4.1 Cardiovascular Disease
COPD patients have 2-3x higher cardiovascular risk:
- **Hypertension (I10):** Lisinopril (314076) or Losartan (310798); Amlodipine (197361) safe to add
- **Heart Failure (I50.9):** Metoprolol (866924) — use **cardioselective beta blocker** at low dose; safe in COPD despite traditional concerns
- **Atherosclerotic HD (I25.10):** Atorvastatin (200031) per standard guidelines
- *NOTE:* Avoid non-selective beta blockers in COPD

### 4.2 Osteoporosis Risk
- Increased risk from: chronic ICS use, physical inactivity, smoking
- DEXA scan recommended after cumulative ICS use > 3 years
- Calcium + Vitamin D supplementation

### 4.3 Depression/Anxiety (F32.9)
- Depression prevalence in COPD: **25-40%**
- Screen with PHQ-9 at every visit
- **Sertraline 50 MG (312938)** — preferred SSRI (avoid respiratory depressants)
- **Avoid Lorazepam (835564)** in COPD — benzodiazepines can suppress respiratory drive
  - If absolutely needed, lowest dose and short duration only
  - Document risk-benefit discussion

### 4.4 GERD (K21.0)
- GERD exacerbates COPD via microaspiration
- **Omeprazole 20 MG (311700)** — treat aggressively
- Lifestyle modifications: elevate HOB, avoid late meals

### 4.5 Pain Management
- **Acetaminophen 500 MG (313782)** — preferred first-line analgesic
- **Ibuprofen 200 MG (310965)** — use with caution (renal, GI risk in elderly COPD patients)
- **Hydrocodone/APAP (856987)** — AVOID if possible in COPD (respiratory depression risk)
  - If required: lowest dose, shortest duration, monitor SpO2
  - Document pulmonary risk assessment

## 5. Acute Exacerbation Management (J44.1)

### 5.1 Exacerbation Severity
| Severity | Features | Setting |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Mild | Increased dyspnea, increased sputum, managed with rescue inhaler | Outpatient (CPT 99213-99215) |
| Moderate | Requires systemic steroids and/or antibiotics | Outpatient or ED (CPT 99283-99285) |
| Severe | ED visit or hospitalization required | ED → Inpatient (CPT 99221-99223) |

### 5.2 ED/Inpatient Management (CPT 99284-99285)
1. **Nebulized Albuterol** — continuous or Q2-4 hours
2. **Systemic corticosteroids** — Prednisone 40 mg × 5 days
3. **Antibiotics if purulent sputum:**
   - **Amoxicillin 500 MG (308182)** — first-line for mild-moderate
   - **Azithromycin 250 MG (197511)** — alternative, especially if atypical suspected
4. **Supplemental O2** — target SpO2 88-92% (avoid hyperoxia in CO2 retainers)
5. **Labs:** CBC (85025), CMP (80053), ABG
6. **Imaging:** Chest X-ray (71046) to rule out pneumonia (J18.9)
7. **Non-invasive ventilation (BiPAP)** if: respiratory acidosis (pH < 7.35), severe dyspnea, accessory muscle use

### 5.3 Admission Criteria
- Failure to respond to initial ED treatment within 2-4 hours
- Severe dyspnea or accessory muscle use
- New hypoxemia (SpO2 < 88% on room air)
- Respiratory acidosis
- Significant comorbidities (CHF I50.9, pneumonia J18.9)
- Inadequate home support (assess SDOH factors)

### 5.4 Discharge from Exacerbation
- Bronchodilators Q4-6 hours (not more frequent than Q2 hours)
- Tolerating oral medications
- O2 requirement stable or at baseline
- **Verify inhaler technique** before discharge
- Oral steroid taper prescribed if applicable
- Follow-up within **7 days** (pulmonology or primary care)
- **COPD Action Plan** reviewed and provided

## 6. COPD Action Plan (Patient Copy)

### GREEN ZONE — Doing Well
- Breathing is normal for you
- You can do your usual activities
- **Continue:** Maintenance inhaler(s) daily, rescue albuterol (895994) as needed
- **Goal:** < 2 rescue uses per week

### YELLOW ZONE — Getting Worse
- More short of breath than usual
- Coughing more, change in sputum color
- Needing rescue albuterol more often
- **Action:** Increase rescue albuterol to Q4 hours, start oral steroid if prescribed, call clinic

### RED ZONE — Emergency
- Severe shortness of breath, cannot speak in full sentences
- Chest pain or rapid heartbeat
- Confusion or extreme drowsiness
- Rescue inhaler not helping
- **Action:** Call 911 or go to nearest ED (FAC001, FAC003, FAC006, FAC008)

## 9. Smoking Cessation

- **Assess tobacco use status at every encounter** — document in encounter note
- Brief intervention: Ask, Advise, Assist, Arrange follow-up
- Pharmacotherapy: NRT, bupropion, or varenicline
- Referral to Michigan Quitline: 1-800-QUIT-NOW
- **Smoking cessation is the ONLY intervention proven to slow FEV1 decline**
- Payer coverage: Most commercial plans (PAY001-005, PAY009) cover cessation aids; Medicare (PAY006/007) covers counseling; Medicaid (PAY008) covers NRT + counseling
