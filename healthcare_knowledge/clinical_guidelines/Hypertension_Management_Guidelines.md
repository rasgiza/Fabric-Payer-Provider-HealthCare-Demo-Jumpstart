# Lakeside Health System — Hypertension (HTN) Management Guidelines

**ICD-10:** I10 — Essential (primary) hypertension
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Review Cycle:** Annual
**Approved By:** Chief Medical Officer, Lakeside Health System
**Applicable Facilities:** All Lakeside facilities (FAC001–FAC008)

---

## 1. Scope

These guidelines are based on the **2017 ACC/AHA/AAPA/ABC/ACPM/AGS/APhA/ASH/ASPC/NMA/PCNA Guideline for the Prevention, Detection, Evaluation, and Management of High Blood Pressure in Adults** (Whelton PK et al., *Hypertension*. 2018;71(6):e13-e115; *J Am Coll Cardiol*. 2018;71(19):e127-e248). They apply to all patients aged 18+ with a diagnosis of essential hypertension (ICD-10: I10) at Lakeside facilities.

## 2. Diagnosis and Staging

### 2.1 Blood Pressure Categories (2017 ACC/AHA)
| Category | Systolic (mmHg) | Diastolic (mmHg) | Action Level |
|----------|----------------|------------------|-------------|
| Normal | < 120 | and < 80 | Annual screening |
| Elevated | 120-129 | and < 80 | Lifestyle modification, recheck 3-6 mo |
| **Stage 1 HTN** | 130-139 | or 80-89 | Lifestyle + pharmacotherapy if ASCVD risk ≥ 10% or comorbid CKD/DM/HF |
| **Stage 2 HTN** | ≥ 140 | or ≥ 90 | Lifestyle + 2-drug pharmacotherapy from different classes |
| Hypertensive crisis | > 180 | and/or > 120 | Urgent/emergent evaluation |

### 2.2 Treatment Targets
- **Primary target for most adults with HTN:** < 130/80 mmHg (Class I, Level B-R recommendation per Whelton et al. 2018).
- **Adults ≥ 65 with HTN:** < 130 mmHg systolic (Class I, Level A) — non-institutionalized, ambulatory, community-dwelling.
- **HTN + DM or CKD:** < 130/80 mmHg.
- **HTN + HFrEF:** Co-manage per CHF guideline; target < 130/80 with GDMT.

### 2.3 Required Workup at Diagnosis
- BP measurement: average of ≥ 2 readings on ≥ 2 occasions
- Out-of-office confirmation: home BP monitoring (HBPM) or 24h ambulatory BP monitoring (ABPM) — preferred per Whelton et al. 2018, §5
- Labs: BMP (CPT 80048), lipid panel (CPT 80061), urinalysis, HbA1c, TSH
- ECG (CPT 93000) at baseline

## 3. First-Line Pharmacotherapy

For non-Black adults without comorbidities, first-line classes (any of):

### 3.1 Thiazide Diuretics
- **Hydrochlorothiazide 25 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 310798)
  - Starting dose: 12.5-25 mg daily
  - Monitor: K+, Na+, Cr at 1-2 weeks then every 6 months

### 3.2 ACE Inhibitors / ARBs
- **Lisinopril 10 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 314076) — preferred when CKD or DM coexist
  - Starting dose: 10 mg daily, titrate to 40 mg
- **Losartan 50 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 310798) — alternative if ACE-induced cough

### 3.3 Calcium Channel Blockers
- **Amlodipine 5 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 197361) — preferred first-line for Black adults without HF or CKD (per Whelton et al. 2018, §9.2)
  - Starting dose: 5 mg daily, titrate to 10 mg

### 3.4 Combination Therapy
- For Stage 2 HTN: **start two agents from different classes simultaneously** (Class I, Level C-EO).
- Preferred two-drug initial regimens: ACE/ARB + thiazide, or ACE/ARB + CCB.
- **Do not combine ACE inhibitor with ARB** — increased adverse events without added benefit (per Whelton et al. 2018, §9.2.2).

## 4. Adherence and Follow-Up

### 4.1 Adherence Standards
- Target PDC ≥ 0.80 for antihypertensive medications (PQA Antihypertensive PDC measure; aligns with NCQA HEDIS *Statin Therapy / RAS-Antagonists* family).
- Per Burnier & Egan (*Circulation Research* 2019;124(7):1124-1140), ~50% of HTN patients are non-adherent at 1 year — **adherence assessment is mandatory at every visit**.
- Patients with PDC < 0.50 (Non-Adherent) require:
  - Medication therapy management (MTM) review — see `Medication_Therapy_Management_Protocol.md`
  - Consider single-pill combination therapy to reduce pill burden
  - SDOH screen (cost, pharmacy access, transportation) — see `clinical_guidelines/Readmission_Prevention_Protocol.md` §3
  - CCM enrollment if ≥ 2 chronic conditions — see `quality_measures/Chronic_Care_Management_Program.md`

### 4.2 Follow-Up Cadence
| BP Status | Follow-Up Interval |
|-----------|-------------------|
| At goal, stable | Every 3-6 months |
| Newly initiated/uptitrated | 2-4 weeks |
| Stage 2 uncontrolled | Every 2 weeks until controlled |
| Resistant HTN (≥ 3 agents) | Cardiology referral |

### 4.3 Resistant Hypertension
Defined as BP > 130/80 on ≥ 3 antihypertensives at maximally tolerated doses, one of which is a diuretic (per Carey RM et al., *Hypertension* 2018;72(5):e53-e90).
- Add **Spironolactone 25 MG Oral Tablet** (RxNorm 313096) as 4th agent — per PATHWAY-2 trial (Williams B et al., *Lancet* 2015;386(10008):2059-2068).
- Evaluate for secondary causes (renal artery stenosis, primary aldosteronism, OSA).

## 5. Quality Measure Alignment

| Measure | Owner | Threshold | Lakeside Target |
|---------|-------|-----------|----------------|
| HEDIS *Controlling High Blood Pressure (CBP)* | NCQA | BP < 140/90 (commercial), < 140/90 (Medicare ≥ 60) | ≥ 75% |
| PQA *Proportion of Days Covered — Antihypertensives* | PQA | PDC ≥ 0.80 | ≥ 80% |
| CMS Star Ratings *Controlling Blood Pressure* | CMS | Same as HEDIS CBP | 4-star threshold |

Non-adherence on antihypertensives directly degrades the HEDIS CBP rate and is a leading cause of preventable cardiovascular readmissions — see `quality_measures/Readmission_Penalty_Program.md`.
